About the Book

Three Men in a Boat (To Say Nothing of the Dog) is a humorous travelogue written by English writer Jerome K Jerome, first published in 1889. The book follows three friends - Jerome, George, and Harris - and their dog Montmorency as they embark on a boating trip along the River Thames from Kingston to Oxford. Along the way, they encounter various mishaps and comedic situations, and reflect on life, love, and the joys of idleness.

With its witty observations, clever wordplay, and gentle satire, Three Men in a Boat has become a classic of English literature, beloved by readers of all ages and backgrounds.
